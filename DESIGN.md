## Problem Statement

Agam Arora is a 12+ year AI product leader whose current personal website (agamarora.com) has the right visual design but the wrong experience. The site reads like every other PM portfolio: bento grid, proof badges, company logos, "Built GenAI that Shipped." It doesn't communicate what makes Agam different. The target audience, recruiters and hiring managers at top B2B SaaS companies (Stripe, Anthropic, Linear, Figma, Datadog), spends <60 seconds on a portfolio before deciding to dig deeper or bounce. The current site gives them nothing in those 60 seconds that they haven't seen 50 times this week.

## What Makes This Cool

The site is a scroll-driven cinematic that tells Agam's career as a character progression story. A figure starts as a curious kid, grows through career chapters, levels up skills, and collects badges. The first 60 seconds are pure animation, the hook. Then the site itself "grows up" and transitions into a structured professional portfolio, the substance. The meta-narrative: the character matures, and so does the site.

The recruiter doesn't read a resume. They experience one. And the craft of the experience is itself the strongest line on the resume.

**The core insight**: Gamified portfolios optimize for the player. This optimizes for the recruiter's 60-second decision window. The scroll animation isn't a game to play. It's the fastest way to SHOW 12 years of compound skill-building in under a minute.

## Constraints

- Must work on desktop and mobile (recruiters use both)
- Must load fast: <2s first paint, Act 1 interactive within 4s
- Must be accessible: `prefers-reduced-motion` static fallback, semantic HTML, screen reader support
- No UI framework (React/Vue/Svelte). Vanilla JS + Vite bundler + GSAP animation library.
- GSAP used under its free public-site license; site must be publicly deployed.
- Dark-first design (Linear aesthetic), light theme optional
- Netlify deployment (no infrastructure changes)
- Character art: SVG-based, abstract/geometric. No literal cartoon boy. Shapes that evolve.
- Portfolio section (post-animation) must have real substance: case studies with metrics

## Premises

1. Primary audience: recruiters/hiring managers at top B2B SaaS companies. They spend <60 seconds on a portfolio before deciding to dig deeper or bounce.
2. The "growing up" character animation is the narrative spine. A figure leveling up through career stages, gaining skills and badges. This is the unique differentiator.
3. Design language: Apple (scroll-driven reveal) + Linear (dark, confident, fast). Premium and engineered, not whimsical.
4. Current visual design is worth evolving, but UX flow and information architecture need a complete rethink.
5. Any AI/conversational layer should be designed from experience backwards, not from existing tech forwards. Deferred to Phase 2.

## Approaches Considered

### Approach A: The Origin Story
Single-page scroll-driven cinematic. Character grows from kid to AI product leader. One continuous scroll, no pages. AI layer at the end.
- Effort: L | Risk: Medium
- Pros: Genuinely original, emotional, memorable
- Cons: Needs illustration, heavy animation, one bad transition ruins it

### Approach B: The Product Demo
Site IS a SaaS product. Recruiter "onboards" through career. Skills = features. Case studies = docs.
- Effort: M | Risk: Low
- Pros: On-brand for PM, clever inversion, easier to build
- Cons: Could feel gimmicky if metaphor overloaded

### Approach C: The Cinematic Scroll (CHOSEN)
Hybrid. Origin story scroll for the first 60 seconds (hook), then transitions into structured portfolio (substance). The character grows up, and so does the site.
- Effort: XL (Act 1 animation + Act 2 portfolio + transition engineering) | Risk: Medium-High
- Pros: Maximum impact. Hook + depth. Transition IS the metaphor.
- Cons: Hardest to build. Transition is the riskiest single element. Art pipeline is a hard dependency.

## Recommended Approach

**Approach C: The Cinematic Scroll**

The site has two acts. Phase 1 ships Act 1 + Act 2. Phase 2 adds the AI conversational layer.

---

### Act 1: The Hook (0-60 seconds of scroll)

A scroll-driven cinematic powered by GSAP ScrollTrigger. The recruiter scrolls and a character's life unfolds. **4 chapters.** Each chapter spans ~3 viewport heights (roughly 1500px on 1080p), giving ~15 seconds at typical scroll speed. Total Act 1 scroll track: ~12vh (6000px).

**Loading state:** Dark background (#111) with centered site name in Roboto. First SVG frame renders at first paint. GSAP initializes and animation becomes scrollable at "interactive." No spinner, no progress bar.

**Chapter 1 — Curiosity + Data (early years)**
- A small abstract geometric figure appears. Simple shapes: circle (head), line (body).
- Data patterns materialize around it: numbers, small chart shapes, grid lines.
- Text fades in: a short line about the early obsession with data and patterns.
- Skill badges animate in: "Data Science" "Analytics"
- Company logo ghost: AbsolutData

**Chapter 2 — Product (mid career)**
- The figure grows taller, gains more geometric complexity. Wireframe rectangles orbit it.
- The data elements from Chapter 1 don't disappear. They integrate into the wireframes.
- Skill badges: "Product Strategy" "B2B SaaS" "Enterprise"
- Company logos: FarEye, Aroma Magic

**Chapter 3 — AI + Leadership (current era)**
- The figure is fully formed. Maximum geometric complexity. Neural network node patterns, flowing lines suggesting LLM token streams.
- All previous elements visible: data + product + AI unified.
- Skill badges: "GenAI Products" "Multi-Agent Systems" "Global Teams"
- Company logo: AIonOS
- The "unicorn" moment: all skill layers visible at once.

**Chapter 4 — The Transition**
- The figure pauses. All elements hold position.
- Over 300px of scroll distance (approximately 500ms at typical scroll speed): the Act 1 container's opacity fades from 1 to 0. Simultaneously, the Act 2 container fades from opacity 0 to 1. Both containers are positioned in the same viewport space using CSS `position: fixed` during the crossfade.
- GSAP ScrollTrigger pinning is released at this boundary. `scroll-snap-type: y mandatory` activates for Act 2.
- Rationale for snap behavior: ScrollTrigger hijacks scroll position during pinned sequences in Act 1, which conflicts with CSS scroll-snap. Snap is only safe after the pin releases.

---

### Act 2: The Substance (exploration)

Clean, dark, Linear-style portfolio. Standard scrolling. Semantic HTML.

**Section: Impact**
- 2-3 product case studies. Each is a teardown:
  - The problem (1 sentence)
  - The data that informed the decision (specific metrics)
  - The tradeoff weighed
  - The outcome (real numbers)
- Format: card per case study, expandable for detail.

**Section: The Lab**
- Projects. Shararat Voice AI + future projects.
- Each project: what, why, what I learned, try it.

**Section: Contact**
- Clean CTA. Email link + LinkedIn + GitHub. One line: "Let's talk."
- No AI chat in Phase 1. No family section in Phase 1 (defer to Phase 2 or footer line).

### The Act 1 → Act 2 Transition (detailed spec)

This is the highest-risk element. **Must be prototyped first before any content or art work.**

**DOM structure:**
```
<div id="act1" style="position: fixed; inset: 0; z-index: 10;">
  <!-- GSAP-animated SVG canvas -->
</div>
<div id="act2" style="opacity: 0;">
  <!-- Standard scrolling portfolio -->
</div>
```

**Scroll mechanics:**
1. Act 1 is pinned via `ScrollTrigger.create({ pin: "#act1", ... })`. The page scrolls behind it while GSAP animates based on scroll position.
2. At the end of Chapter 3's scroll range, a final ScrollTrigger fires the crossfade:
   - `#act1` animates `opacity: 1 → 0` over 500ms of scroll distance
   - `#act2` animates `opacity: 0 → 1` over the same range
   - When complete: `#act1` gets `display: none`, `#act2` gets `position: relative`
3. ScrollTrigger pinning releases. Normal scroll behavior resumes. `scroll-snap-type` activates.

**Failure mode:** If the crossfade feels janky, fallback is a hard cut: `#act1` disappears, `#act2` appears. A clean cut is better than a broken transition.

### Technical Architecture

**Animation Engine:**
- GSAP 3.x + ScrollTrigger plugin
- SVG character art: pure geometric shapes, animated via GSAP. No Lottie. SVG keyframing with GSAP is sufficient for abstract geometry, and avoids the 300KB Lottie dependency.
- If SVG complexity exceeds what GSAP can handle smoothly (>60fps on a 2020 laptop), simplify the art, don't add Lottie.

**Build:**
- Vite (zero-config mode) for ES module bundling, tree-shaking GSAP, and dev server
- Output: static HTML/CSS/JS bundle → Netlify
- This is not "adding a framework." It's using standard tooling to ship clean code.

**Asset Budget:**

| Asset | Target Size |
|-------|-------------|
| GSAP + ScrollTrigger (tree-shaken) | ~100KB gzipped |
| SVG character art (all chapters) | ~50KB total |
| CSS (main + lab) | ~20KB |
| JS (application code) | ~15KB |
| Fonts (Roboto 400+600) | ~40KB |
| **Act 1 total** | **~225KB** |
| Act 2 images (lazy-loaded) | ~500KB |
| **Full page weight** | **~725KB** |

Act 1 is interactive at ~225KB. Full page stays under 1MB.

**Performance:**
- Critical path: HTML → CSS → GSAP → first SVG frame. Target: <2s on 4G.
- Act 2 content lazy-loads while the user scrolls Act 1 (~15-60s of buffer time).
- Fonts: `font-display: swap` to avoid FOIT.

**Responsive:**

| Viewport | Act 1 | Act 2 |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Desktop (1024px+) | Full GSAP animation, all chapters | Full portfolio layout |
| Tablet (768-1024px) | Simplified: fewer orbiting elements, same narrative | 2-column grid |
| Mobile (<768px) | **Static card sequence**: one card per chapter, each containing the text + skill badges + a single SVG frame (no animation). Fade-in on scroll via IntersectionObserver, not GSAP. | Single column |

Mobile gets the same narrative in a different format. No animation means no jank, no battery drain, no iOS Safari scroll issues.

**Accessibility:**
- `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`: Act 1 renders as static cards (same as mobile). Each card shows the final keyframe SVG of that chapter (the most complete visual state). No animation. All content accessible.
- Semantic HTML: `<article>` for each chapter, `<section>` for Act 2 sections, proper heading hierarchy.
- `aria-hidden="true"` on decorative SVG elements. All meaningful text in the DOM.
- Skip-to-content link targeting Act 2.

**Error handling:**
- GSAP fails to load (CDN blocked by corporate firewall): self-hosted fallback bundle via Vite. If both fail: Act 1 renders as static cards (CSS-only, no JS dependency).
- SVG assets fail to load: each chapter has a CSS background color and the text/badges still render. The experience degrades to "text on dark background" — functional, not broken.

**SEO:**
- All text content is in the DOM as semantic HTML (not injected by JS). Google can crawl it.
- Open Graph + Twitter Card meta tags with proper og:image (screenshot of the site).
- Schema.org JSON-LD (Person) with updated job title, sameAs links.
- Each Act 2 section uses proper `<h2>` headings for search indexability.
- `<noscript>` block with a text summary and link to LinkedIn for crawlers that don't execute JS.

**Events to track:**

| Event | Trigger | Purpose |

## Reviewer Concerns

Issues flagged during adversarial review that remain open (not blocking, but should be addressed during build):

1. **Keyboard navigation for Act 1**: No spec for keyboard-only users during the scroll animation. Should arrow keys advance chapters, or just rely on the skip-to-content link? Decide during Act 1 build.
2. **Font fallback stack**: Define `font-family: 'Roboto', -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', sans-serif` in CSS. Minor but prevents a serif flash if Roboto CDN fails.
3. **Safari momentum scrolling at Act 1→Act 2 boundary**: ScrollTrigger pin release can cause scroll position jumps on Safari. Budget 2-3 days for debugging this (included in Phase 1 timeline).
4. **"Shapes that evolve" needs visual references**: Before building, find 2-3 Dribbble/Behance examples of abstract geometric character art that establish the target aesthetic. This is the art direction step.
5. **GSAP bundle size**: ~100KB is an estimate. Benchmark actual size with Vite tree-shaking during the transition prototype step. Adjust asset budget if needed.
