## 1. Refined statement

The IC (individual contributor) career path is a legitimate endgame for a PM, not a way-station to people-management. People management is "a critical skill but it's not for everyone." Career growth on the functional-expertise track is "not bottlenecked"; the legitimacy claim is altitude-equivalent to managerial growth, not consolation-grade. **Inheritance frame:** Agam carries an explicit family-of-origin / business-family default toward "people management is the end goal." The IC-path belief is the named supersession of that default, not a position adopted from neutral ground.

The belief's load is identity-level, not policy-level: it answers "what *kind* of PM career is Agam playing for?" The answer is depth-and-craft (functional expertise as the growth axis) rather than span-of-control. This is consistent with — and structurally related to — Agam's PM-craft beliefs (`belief.pm-is-featherless-hat`, `belief.pm-is-99-should-we-1-can-we`, `belief.taste-over-execution`), which are all craft-altitude rather than org-altitude claims.
