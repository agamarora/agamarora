## 1. Refined statement (Agam's framing)

**Public form, 2025-12-04:**
> "I am extremely opinionated about adding customization to products. To the point where I feel like I've lost a hard-fought battle with myself if a PRD ends up including customization settings. My brain is wired to jump to the long-term horizon where today's 'super customizable' product eventually requires six months of implementation, an implementation partner, hundreds of training documents, and a roadmap item just to enable a true day-zero go-live."

**What Agam is actually claiming (decomposed):**

1. Customization-in-the-PRD is a **PM craft failure**, not a product feature. The defeat language ("lost a hard-fought battle with myself") signals this is an internal-craft commitment, not a UX preference.
2. The economic argument is **deferred-cost**, not present-cost. Customization is cheap to spec and expensive to live with: it imports an implementation tail (six months, partner, training docs, roadmap drag).
3. The reference frame is **day-zero go-live**. Anti-customization is anti-pre-implementation-tax: every toggle in the PRD is a pre-paid mortgage on every future deployment.
4. The position is **PM-first, not designer-first**. Agam is not arguing UX-minimalism for its own sake (no Dieter Rams citation, no "less is more" aesthetic). He is arguing that the PRD is the contract that locks in deployment cost, and customization in that contract is a self-inflicted wound.
5. The **enemy is the glamorous default**. "Super customizable" appears in scare-quotes — Agam knows it sells in the meeting room. The opinionatedness is needed precisely because saying yes is easier.

**Posture/voice notes:** "Hard-fought battle with myself" is unusually personal craft-language — earlier PM-craft posts (2021-22) use external metaphors (parenting, featherless hat); 2025-12-04 internalizes the craft-as-self frame. Consistent with E4b builder-phase voice (R3d Cluster 16). "My brain is wired to" claims the disposition as innate-to-Agam, not universal PM advice — agent should NOT generalize as "all PRDs should drop customization." The post (~80 words, two paragraphs) closes without prescription, names no counter-PM or product. Opinionatedness declared, not demonstrated against a target.
