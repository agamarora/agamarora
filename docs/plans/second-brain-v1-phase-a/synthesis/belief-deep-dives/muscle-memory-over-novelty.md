## 1. Refined statement

**Canonical (2025-12-26 post — Shreyas Doshi):**
> "While I continue to spend more time practising these, it continues to benefit me to re-watch and re-read some of his material. To get this into my muscle memory."
> "I would recommend most PMs and even general business practitioners to do this as well. Use this man's writing/talks as not just one-offs, but commit to them."

**Refined (R4 statement):** Once you have chosen a framework or concept worth internalizing, the work is not done at first reading. Craft at PM-level requires deliberate repetition — re-watching, re-reading, and practicing until the concept operates below conscious awareness. The prescription is not novelty-seeking (adding new frameworks) but depth-drilling (deepening the ones already selected). Hours put in is the only LCD. You cannot skim on it.

**The two-move sequence this belief prescribes:**
1. Choose the right concept (that's `belief.learn-concepts-not-tools` — what to study)
2. Commit to it via repetition until it reaches instinct level (that's this belief — how to study)

## 3. Counter-evidence

**Tension 1: "Be curious, keep experimenting" register**

Multiple posts in the AI-stack / tinkering lineage use language like "Be curious, keep experimenting" (2024-09-17 Groq comparator close, 2024-06-05 ollama post close) and "Have tried almost every framework out there from Autogpt to Autogen to CrewAI" (2024-07-24). This appears to endorse novelty-chasing over repetition.

Resolution: The domain differs. The "try everything" posture applies to *AI technology evaluation* — frameworks, models, tooling — where the landscape is genuinely novel and rapid evaluation is necessary. The muscle-memory-over-novelty belief applies to *PM craft concepts* (communication, frameworks, principles) where the underlying concepts are stable (STP is 60 years old; Doshi's communication framing will not be superseded by a new model release). Agam operates two learning modes: breadth-exploration in technology (because it moves fast and triage requires sampling), and depth-drilling in craft (because the concepts are stable and mastery requires repetition). They are coherent, not contradictory.

**Tension 2: "I am all for continuous learning" (2024-07 Collab, approx)**

The Collab item "take small steps, set small goals and appreciate small wins... learning one day at a time" could read as endorsing incremental novelty — always learning the next new thing. Closer read: it's a *pace* prescription (small steps, don't be overwhelmed), not a *content* prescription. Continuous learning is about pace and consistency, not about novelty-seeking over repetition. Compatible with the belief.

**Tension 3: "This role requires a lot of ongoing deep learning" — ongoing implies new**

From the tech-PM Collab article (2024-06-14): "you can't be done with the studies ever in this role." This could read as perpetual novelty acquisition. But the context is ongoing *depth* in the PM craft — going deeper with engineers, not hopping to new frameworks. The ongoing learning is vertical (depth), not horizontal (novelty).

**Verdict:** No genuine contradictions. The counter-evidence resolves into a two-mode model: breadth/exploration in technology (fast-moving, novelty-driven triage needed), depth/repetition in PM craft concepts (stable, mastery-driven). The belief applies specifically to the PM-craft domain.
