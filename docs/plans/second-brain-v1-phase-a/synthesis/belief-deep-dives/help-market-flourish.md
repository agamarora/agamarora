## 1. Refined statement

**Help the market flourish, don't dominate.** Choose collaborative-growth ("increase the size of the pie") over zero-sum conquest ("kill everyone out there and crush your market"). Domination has a cost — moral, emotional, financial, even aesthetic. The 2017-09-14 post is the inversion: Agam watches a Grant Cardone "Dominate, don't compete" video, is briefly seduced ("hell yeah, dominate"), then publicly walks it back in the same post. The walk-back is the belief.

This is a **market-posture** belief — how Agam orients to competition, opportunity, and gain — paired with the **epistemic-posture** sibling `belief.never-be-smartest-in-room`. Together they form Agam's twin postures: don't try to be the biggest, don't try to be the smartest. Both bias toward expanding the room rather than ranking within it.

Refined ontology slug holds: `belief.help-market-flourish`. The 2018-07-12 post canonicalizes the phrasing ("go increase the size of the pie if you want a bigger share") as the durable mnemonic. The 2017-09-14 post is the *origin event*; 2018-07-12 is the *crystallized form*.
