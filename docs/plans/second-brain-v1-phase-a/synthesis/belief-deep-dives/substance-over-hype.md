## 1. Refined statement

**Reduce the hyped category to its substrate, then evaluate.** When a technology, framework, or product convention arrives wearing a brand new label, Agam strips it back to what it actually does at the layer below: "blockchain is a database innovation first," "agents are LLMs with tools and memory," "GenAI is a platform revolution because it reduces the cost of cognition." Substrate-evaluation precedes excitement. If the substrate is genuinely new (the cost of X collapses to near-zero), the hype is earned and Agam will press on it. If the substrate is the same technology renamed (ICO is not product, "AI customization" is not value), Agam refuses the frame in writing.

This is the candidate ROOT disposition because it is not a topical position. It is a posture toward new categories that gets re-applied across hype cycles 8 years apart, and survives Agam being personally invested in the category being evaluated.

## 3. Counter-evidence

The disposition is real but it is not a blanket cynicism — Agam DOES get excited about specific technologies, and the lens for distinguishing his excitement from "hype" is whether the substrate-test passes. Three pieces of counter-evidence complicate the belief if read uncharitably; on closer read they confirm the substrate-test rather than violate it.

1. **2018 blockchain enthusiasm clusters.** In the same window where Agam writes "blockchain is a database innovation first" (3 Mar 2018), he is also publishing #blockchain hashtag posts (2018-02-22 hiring-Solidity, 2018-02-23 cryptocurrency-portfolio, 2018-03-08 World 4.0 Women's Day greeting tagged blockchain, 2018-04-something governance post — multiple cheerleading-adjacent posts). Read uncharitably: Agam was a blockchain hype-rider in 2018 who only later distanced himself. Read charitably: he was selling V2 Games product, working in the category, AND writing the substrate-honesty posts FROM INSIDE the category. The 2018-03-06 "biggest irony" post is not a critic outside the cycle — it is a builder inside the cycle naming what the cycle gets wrong. This is the strongest evidence that substance-over-hype is genuinely his disposition rather than retrospective positioning: he wrote the substrate-reduction WHILE financially exposed to the hype. Counter-evidence is real but reframes the belief positively, not negatively.

2. **2024-08-30 GameNGen post · `urn:li:activity:7235123739565158402`** — "Google's research team has made yet another breakthrough with a new research called GameNGen...GameNGen is truly a breakthrough. Super times." Two clear "breakthrough" usages plus a heart-emoji "Super times" sign-off. Reads as enthusiasm-without-substrate-test. But: this is the same post where Agam pairs GameNGen with the GPT-4 cost reduction (36 USD to 4 USD per million tokens) and explicitly calls the cost reduction "a testament to commoditisation" — applying the "cost of X collapses" substrate-test to the price drop. The breakthrough framing is reserved for the research artifact (game-from-policy is a substantive capability shift), the commoditisation framing is reserved for the pricing. Both pieces pass a substrate-test in his own framework. Not violation — refinement.

3. **2024-09-13 OpenAI o1-preview post (Kahneman System-1/System-2 framing)** — Agam endorses o1-preview enthusiastically: "If the positioning of o1-preview is in line with the product capabilities, we are looking at another breakthrough for agentic systems which relied on the Thinking-Reasoning-Planning-Iterating loop." Note the conditional "If the positioning is in line with the product capabilities" — Agam couches the breakthrough claim BEHIND a substrate-test he cannot yet run (he does not have access). This is anti-hype hedging built into excited language, not a violation.

**Net counter-evidence:** Three candidate violations all turn out to encode a substrate-test, either explicit ("If positioning aligns...") or implicit (separating breakthrough-research from commodity-pricing). The disposition is consistent, not selectively applied.

**One genuine wrinkle:** the 2018 #blockchain hashtag posts that have NO substrate framing (e.g. 2018-02-22 hiring-Solidity-rockstars, 2018-02-23 cryptocurrency-portfolio "magical returns") look like ordinary E2-era promotional voice. These are best read as company-promotion (hiring posts, market commentary) rather than belief-claims. They sit alongside the substrate-reduction posts without contradicting them, which is itself characteristic of a young Agam who can hold both registers (sell the product / question the category) without internal conflict.

## 4. Belief history

### Origin
**2018-02-10** — "Blockchain = Gamification decentralized." Earliest visible substrate-rename move. The disposition exists in nascent form (terse, slightly snarky, no theory yet — just a one-line reduction).

### Crystallization moment
**2018-03-04 to 2018-03-06** — Two-day window. The 3 Mar post locks the phrase "a database innovation first." The 6 Mar post (one of the few 2018 posts the master-belief-list cites by name) extends to a meta-claim about cognitive failure: "No one wants to accept the biggest database innovation as just a database innovation." This is when the disposition acquires both a substrate-reduction tool ("X is Y first") AND a meta-frame (the industry refuses substrate framing because hype demands novelty). Both halves recur for 8 years.

### Refinement(s)
- **2024-03-31 (Devin):** Adds the "I would catch on to the hype once X" rhetorical move — withhold-belief-pending-substrate-test. Explicit conditional, not just retrospective reduction.
- **2024-07-12 (4th-platform-revolution):** Generalizes the substrate-test into a definition: "Technology that radically reduces the cost of something." This is the most explicit substrate-test in the corpus. Also the post where Agam self-callbacks to blockchain ("I am looking at you Blockchain") — confirms the through-line is intentional self-tracking.
- **2024-09-04 (9 takeaways):** Anti-hype operationalized as field data. The substrate-test becomes a forecasting tool ("honeymoon period → consolidation → governance"). Refined from "this category is overhyped" to "the cycle has a predictable shape and I can name where we are in it."
- **2025-12-04 (anti-customization):** Refined onto product-craft surface. Substrate-test applied to PM-internal hype-words (customization, configurability, optionality). Anti-hype is now a craft heuristic, not just a category-evaluation lens.
- **2026-04-09 (Spec > Sprint trilogy):** Refined to a 30-minute test cycle. Substrate-evaluation can happen on a single tool in a single afternoon. Operationalized to the maximum.

### Current form (2026)
The disposition is now a fully-formed evaluation procedure: (1) when a new category lands, name its substrate ("X is Y at heart"); (2) ask if the substrate genuinely reduces the cost of something; (3) if yes, press on it; (4) if no, refuse the frame regardless of incentive. Most recent expression is the 2026-04-09 Google Stitch post — 30 minutes, fails substrate-test, dropped.

### Supersession status
- **Does not supersede a prior belief.** No earlier "I trust the hype" stance exists in the corpus to displace.
- **Is not superseded by any later belief.** The disposition is unchanged across 8 years; what changes is only the surface area it gets applied to.
- **Is the parent of `belief.ico-is-funding-not-product`** (the 2018-02-15 post that became its own slug). ICO-is-funding is substance-over-hype applied to a single hyped category at a single moment. The parent disposition outlives the child belief.

**Recommendation: Option A.** The 8-year through-line evidence (4 distinct hype cycles, same response pattern) makes substance-over-hype unique among Agam's beliefs. Nothing else in the master-list spans 2018 → 2026 with the same shape. Wiki should reflect that.
