## 3. Counter-evidence — moments of passive consumption

The instrumental stance is durable. The clearest counter-evidence is *category-routine* not *belief-contradicting*.

**Bucket 1 — "Commenting for reach" tag-comments (genuine drive-by participation).**

The comments corpus contains multiple Agam comments that read literally "Commenting for reach" on connection posts (e.g., 2024-05-31 Sneha Sharma thread). These ARE passive in the sense that they're network-maintenance gestures, not original contribution. But they're counter-evidence in only the weakest sense:
- Agam is *deliberately* surfacing the tag-comment mechanic. It's not unconscious feed-scrolling.
- The text "Commenting for reach" is itself instrumental — he's transparent about the mechanic, same as in 2017 ("post with likes + images will spread pretty far").
- The same disposition (algorithm-as-known-system, transparency-about-the-mechanic) governs both the deep posts AND the drive-by comments.

So: not a belief-contradiction, but a frequency-floor — even Agam's lowest-effort LinkedIn output is instrumental, just at the trivial end of the spectrum.

**Bucket 2 — Endorsement-comments without original substance.**

A non-trivial fraction of the comments corpus is "Thanks for sharing X, great point" type endorsement-comments on others' posts. These are network-warmth gestures, not opinion-output. Counter-evidence to the *thinking-out-loud* sub-claim: Agam isn't always thinking when he posts. Sometimes he's just maintaining the relationship.

But this is consistent with his 2024-05-23 frame ("the true power of human connections"). Connection-warmth IS one of the platform's instrumental uses; it's not at odds with the belief, it's an application of it.

**Bucket 3 — No "LinkedIn is bad" or "I should leave LinkedIn" register exists in the corpus.**

The closest thing to platform-skepticism is the 2024-04-01 PM-influencer post ("There are more senior PMs selling services to aspiring PMs than in any other job function. Things are becoming too transactional"). This is critique of *user behavior on the platform*, not of the platform itself. Agam never moralizes about LinkedIn-the-system — he's frequently amused by its mechanics, never disgusted with them. The absence of the "I'm taking a break from social media" register is itself evidence: LinkedIn is a tool, you don't need a break from a tool you use deliberately.

**Bucket 4 — No supersession.**

Unlike `belief.horizontal-ai-will-scale` (cleanly killed 2025-06-20) or `belief.dominate-dont-compete` (superseded 2017-09-14), this belief has no supersession edge. The 2024-11-15 certifications post is *adjacent* (different scope: paid credentials, not platform mechanics) and resolves a tension rather than killing the belief. Master-list rates this as "STRENGTHEN" via Cluster 13 + 18 — the stance scales with platform-leverage rather than fading.
