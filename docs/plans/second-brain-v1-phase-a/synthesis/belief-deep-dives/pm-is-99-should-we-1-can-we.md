## 1. Belief statement (canonical + context)

**Canonical statement (verbatim, 2022-06-03 anchor post):**

> "Product management is the grind of answering 99 questions of 'should we do it?' to get to that 1 bonus question of 'can we do it?'.
>
> That 1 is disruption. The rest, food."

— urn:li:activity:6938349828124921856 · 49 reactions · 1 comment

**What the belief actually claims** (parsed from the corpus, not paraphrased outside it):

1. PM work is *predominantly* selection / discrimination, not creation. The ratio is explicitly 99:1.
2. "Should we?" is the load-bearing question; "can we?" is the bonus the team earns by getting "should we?" right.
3. "That 1 is disruption. The rest, food." — when PM gets the rare "can we?" right, the upside is disruption-grade. The 99 grind is what keeps the company alive (food); the 1 is what makes it grow (disruption).
4. The framing is identity-level, not procedural — Agam writes "Product management is" (definitional), not "a good PM should" (advisory).

**Why this is the most quotable PM identity claim in the corpus:**

- It compresses a controversial PM-vs-engineer-vs-designer territorial dispute into 27 words.
- It's an aphorism (Agam's strongest register per R3c) plus a numerical anchor (55%, 5-6 salaries, 99/1 — Agam likes specific numbers).
- It contains the inversion-on-final-clause move R3d Cluster 11 identified as Agam's signature aphorism device ("That 1 is disruption. The rest, food.").
- It is the *only* PM-identity post that uses an explicit ratio. Every other PM-as-X post (featherless hat, parenting, CEO-analogy) uses a metaphor; this one uses a quantification. The rarity of the device matches the badge-grade payoff.
