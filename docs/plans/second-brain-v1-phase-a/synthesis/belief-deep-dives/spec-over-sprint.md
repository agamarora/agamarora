## §4 — Supersession map: ship-fast → spec-over-sprint

This belief is one of the cleanest supersessions in the corpus. The trail:

### 4.1 — `belief.ship-fast` (the predecessor)

**Era:** E2c (2020-05-19). Status: **superseded** in master-belief-list.

**Canonical evidence:**
- **2016-01-04:** "MVP or Minimum Viable Product lets you push into the market faster and leaves a lot of scope for agile development - Lean Startup."
- **2019-03-05:** "MVP - Minimum Viable Product is a framework that has applications in industries as diverse as beauty, wellness and retail."
- **2019-03-07:** "MVP vs MMP — Minimum viable product vs Minimum Marketable Product." (Refining MVP, but still inside the MVP frame.)
- **2020-05-19:** "Sharing that I am ready to take up prototyping projects for cross platform app development...With dart/flutter I am able to create 'quick and dirty', yet 'powerful and clean' looking mobile app prototypes (fully working applications or MVP if you may) to go along a business pitch. No longer 'just an idea!' :)"

**The 2020-05-19 stance:** Speed-to-prototype is the virtue. The phrase "quick and dirty, yet powerful and clean" is the apotheosis — Agam celebrating that he can ship fast with low overhead. The implicit axiom: ship-fast > don't-ship.

### 4.2 — The flip (what changed)

**The 2026-04-09 post does the supersession explicitly:**
> "Tried and dropped Google Stitch in under 30 minutes."

The 30-minute drop is the inversion of the 2020 quick-and-dirty stance. In 2020, "quick and dirty" was the virtue — get something out the door even if rough. In 2026, "30-minute drop" is the virtue — refuse the quick-and-dirty output because the spec is more valuable than the speed.

**What changed in the AI era to flip iteration economics:**

| Era | Iteration cost | Spec cost | Binding constraint | Therefore |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pre-LLM (2016-2022) | High (days to weeks per iteration) | Moderate (days for a PRD) | **Iteration speed** — getting the next version in front of users | **`belief.ship-fast`** — iterate over spec |
| Post-LLM (2024+) | Near-zero (Google Stitch generates a layout in seconds; Claude Code ships a feature in minutes) | Same as before (still days for a real PRD) | **Spec quality** — the only thing that gates the AI from amplifying garbage | **`belief.spec-over-sprint`** — spec over iterate |

**Why iteration economics flipped:** When iteration was scarce (slow CI, manual coding, week-long design rounds), a fast prototype gave you irreplaceable user-feedback signal. Speed > preparation. When iteration is cheap (LLMs generate full layouts/code/copy in seconds), a fast prototype gives you noise — a generative tool produces "approximate everything" (2026-04-09 phrasing). Now preparation > speed, because the only signal that matters is the spec you locked in beforehand. The model amplifies it.

**The R3d Cluster 16 framing of this flip:** "When you've already spent hours speccing every pixel...a generative tool gives you a worse version of what you've already decided." The implicit second clause: when you HAVEN'T spent hours speccing, the generative tool gives you a better version than you had — but a worse version than you would have had with a real spec. So generative tools are a tax on the un-prepared and a discount on the well-prepared. Spec-over-sprint is the rational response: invest where the leverage is.

### 4.3 — What did NOT get superseded

`belief.ship-the-prototype` (2020-05-19, "Build to validate, don't just conceptualize") is **NOT** superseded. Master-belief-list keeps it as core (5-round). The supersession is precise:

- `belief.ship-fast` (speed > spec) → **superseded** by `belief.spec-over-sprint`.
- `belief.ship-the-prototype` (don't-be-the-just-an-idea-person) → **still held**, refines into "ship the well-spec'd prototype."

**Test:** Agam still ships prototypes — second-brain launch 2026-04-23 ("Plain markdown. Git. Open source. MIT. Free forever.") is shipping; the 2024-2026 tinkering lineage (Cluster 15) is shipping. So ship-the-prototype lives. What died is the "quick and dirty is OK" sub-axiom.

### 4.4 — Supersession edges

```

belief.ship-the-prototype  --[refined-by, 2026-04-09]-->  belief.spec-over-sprint
```

## §6 — The trilogy as one composite stance

**Per master-belief-list:** "taste-over-execution and context-over-prompt are siblings, not children" of spec-over-sprint. R4 confirms this and goes further: the three are **co-equal facets of one stance**.

### 6.1 — Why three lines, not one

Agam could have written "Spec > Sprint" alone. He chose three. The three lines are not a hierarchy — they are three views of the same axiom across three layers:

| Line | Layer | What it says | What it refuses |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Spec > Sprint** | Process layer | The thing you write before building beats the thing you build | "Just ship and iterate" — refuse iteration as the substitute for thinking |
| **Taste > Execution** | Craft layer | The judgment you bring to the artifact beats the artifact's polish | "Just make it look good" — refuse polish as the substitute for taste |
| **Context > Prompt** | Tooling layer | The context you feed the model beats the prompt you write to it | "Just craft a clever prompt" — refuse prompt-cleverness as the substitute for context-richness |

**The composite axiom:** Across all three layers, the leverage is in what you bring TO the work, not in the work-velocity itself. Preparation > execution.

### 6.2 — Why siblings, not parent-child

R4 considered spec-over-sprint as parent of the other two. Reject:

- **`belief.taste-over-execution`** (R3d Cluster 16): craft-axis claim. Taste tells you which spec to write. Without taste the spec is wrong; without the spec the taste has no artifact. Each presupposes the other. Sibling.
- **`belief.context-over-prompt`** (R3d Cluster 2): tooling-axis claim. Context is what the spec becomes when handed to an LLM; the prompt is the I/O seam. Context-over-prompt is spec-over-sprint applied to LLM tooling. Sibling — slightly closer to spec-over-sprint than taste-over-execution, but still co-equal.

**Ontology shape:**

```
                  theme.spec-first-taste
              /            |             \
spec-over-sprint   taste-over-execution   context-over-prompt
 (process layer)    (craft layer)          (tooling layer)
              \            |             /
               [composite, 2026-04-09 trilogy — siblings, co-equal]
```

### 6.3 — Metaphor-stack-to-axiom progression

Cluster 6 shows Agam built TOWARD the trilogy through metaphors: 2021-07-12 PM-as-parenting → 2021-09-22 featherless-hat → 2022-06-03 99/1 grind → 2025-11-25 CEO-analogy → 2025-12-04 anti-customization → 2025-12-26 muscle-memory → 2026-04-09 trilogy. Twelve years of building the disposition; one post that names it. R3d: "first explicit taste-first axiom." R4 extends: also the **first explicit spec-first axiom**. Both axioms crystallize in the same post — that's why they're siblings.

### 6.4 — Wiki implication

ONE theme page (`theme.spec-first-taste`) opening with the trilogy as composite stance, then sections per layer. NOT three separate pages. The agent invoking any one of the three should ground in the same disposition — only the layer differs.

Spec-over-sprint applies when iteration is cheap (post-LLM). Agam still holds build-measure-learn (core, 3-round) for domains where iteration economics haven't flipped — physical products, regulated industries, hardware. R4 recommends ontology edges: `build-measure-learn --[applies-when-iteration-is-scarce]-->` and `spec-over-sprint --[applies-when-iteration-is-cheap]-->`.
