# AI fluency is table stakes for PMs, not a differentiator

## Statement

AI fluency is now table stakes for PMs. Table stakes in the literal poker sense: not a flex, not a differentiator, a non-negotiable buy-in. The relevant archetype of AI PM is the application-layer one, using existing models to unlock growth, not building the models.

Three load-bearing parts:

1. Discipline-level: PMs need to be as fluent in AI tools as they are in PowerPoint and Excel.
2. Practitioner-level: "As a product manager, I find myself using ChatGPT for just about everything except product management - and it feels like the perfect fit." AI as cognitive scaffolding around PM craft, not a replacement for it.
3. Archetype split: there are two kinds of AI PMs. Foundation-layer PMs work at labs improving the models. Application-layer PMs use existing models to unlock growth. The application layer has higher demand, more open use-cases, and is where the craft of PM taste actually applies.

The second point still holds in 2026. AI tools are inside execution: thinking-as-execution, writing, coding, PRDs. AI does not replace taste. Taste of what to build, what experience, what value is the irreducible PM job. The 2023-03-14 line named where taste lives, and taste has not moved.

## Origin

The belief crystallized in a three-week window in early 2023. The manifesto post (2023-03-14) lists six concrete uses: data analysis with Jupyter, Pandas, SQL, marketing copy, structuring thoughts and notes, building a personal website, PRD refinement, brainstorming, home-lab setup. The post closes: "To me, tis but an extension." That is the original framing.

Five days later (2023-03-19): "Modern jobs will soon require us to be as fluent in AI tools like Midjourney, ChatGPT, and others, as we are in traditional software." The PowerPoint and Excel analogy makes table stakes intuitive without requiring the phrase.

The archetype split arrived on 2024-03-28 in a Collab Article response: "1/ AI PMs working on improving the AI models (part of openai, anthropic etc) / 2/ AI PMs using existing AI models to unlock growth for their product. I feel the second one would have higher demand given that we are still figuring out all use cases." Application-layer from this date forward.

## Refinement arc

The belief matured through several substrate shifts. Each shift changed what fluency consists of; none changed the disposition.

2023-03-14: chat-LLM fluency. Data analysis, writing, structuring. Six concrete surfaces.

2024 Collab Article grind (top 1-2% globally in both AI and Product Management): "Being technical is not about knowing a technology but using the technology. Play with APIs when you are bored." Applied-not-studied. Table-stakes means hands-on, not certified.

2024-07-04: AI Product Manager as job title at an enterprise voice AI platform. Considerable GenAI product concepts built and demoed to enterprise customers. Fluency is now industrial-scale.

2025-06-20: agent-first manifesto. Agam no longer argues for AI fluency; he assumes it and reasons from the next layer up. Table-stakes is the floor on which agent-first thinking stands.

2026-04-09: "Spec > Sprint / Taste > Execution / Context > Prompt." Differentiation is no longer "are you using the tools" but "do you have the taste to spec what the tools should do." AI tools handle the grunt. Taste is what remains irreducibly PM.

2026 reframe: AI is inside execution. Outsource the grunt. Taste of what to build is outside AI's reach. The 2023 line held because it named where taste lives.
