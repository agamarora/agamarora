# Learn concepts, not tools

## Statement

Strive to learn concepts and not tools. Be a creater.

Frameworks endure; tools rotate. The underlying concept - STP, JTBD, MVP, AARRR, STAR, V/V/U - stays load-bearing across decades. The specific tool that runs any given concept is a replaceable substrate. STP (formalized by Kotler from Wendell Smith's 1956 work) is still live in 2024 AI-PM practice. The concept has not changed; the tools running segmentation have changed dozens of times.

The analogy: three kinds of people. A driver uses the tool. A mechanic can repair it. An engineer knows the tool inside-out and can create similar tools from the concept. The world does not need more drivers. AI makes drivers redundant.

## Origin

December 4, 2019. Agam was going through online courses on digital marketing and noticed the emphasis on marketing tools versus marketing concepts. The driver/mechanic/engineer analogy emerged from that observation. The canonical formulation: "Strive to learn concepts and not tools. Be a creater!"

The "AI will make you redundant if not now but soon" line is part of the same post. Written in 2019 - three years before ChatGPT's public release. The observation was not a prediction made in hindsight.

Nine months earlier, March 2019, the seed was already visible: "I still remember clearly my professor at business school writing the big bold words on the white board." STP as an example of framework endurance. The concept-grounding-via-named-source pattern appears here first - the same pattern that later produces Greene, Kahneman, and Doshi citations.

## Refinement arc

The belief deepened and became more operational across four rounds of evidence.

The Collab Articles grind (November 2023 to July 2024) is where the belief became systematic. Across 11 PM-fundamentals items, Agam reached for named frameworks as the answer architecture: STP for marketing basics, JTBD for customer insight, MVP for stakeholder management, AARRR for growth metrics, STAR for structured framing, NFR for production risk, V/V/U for AI product validation. Not casual flourishes. Framework citations were the answer itself.

The 2024-03-06 "play with APIs when you are bored" piece compressed the same point: "Being technical is not about knowing a technology but using the technology." Concept-application over tool-knowledge in 16 words.

By December 2025, the Shreyas Doshi muscle-memory piece added a practice-cadence layer: "To get this into my muscle memory." The belief moved from declarative ("learn concepts") to operational ("re-engage the same concepts deliberately until they are instinct"). That is the sibling belief `belief.muscle-memory-over-novelty` in the learning loop.

The AI-era pressure strengthened rather than superseded this belief. When ChatGPT arrived and commoditized surface-level breadth, the response was not "abandon frameworks for tools" but "the frameworks matter more now." A Human-GPT can surface any tool. An engineer-grade practitioner can conceive the next one.
