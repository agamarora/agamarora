# Taste over Execution

## Statement

In an era where AI commoditizes execution (writing, coding, PRDs, even rough design), the bottleneck and the differentiator is taste. Knowing what to build, what to ship, and what to leave out is the irreducible PM skill. AI tools handle the grunt; taste is what AI cannot replace. Product management was, is, and will continue to be about the taste of what to build, what experience, and what value. That has not changed. The vocabulary for it arrived in April 2026. The disposition arrived years earlier.

## Origin

The function of taste has a traceable lineage starting in 2021-22, years before the vocabulary. The PM metaphor stack from that era does taste-work without naming it: "Product management is a featherless hat, omnipresent yet indistinguishable if done properly" (2021-09-22). "Product management is the grind of answering 99 questions of 'should we do it?' to get to that 1 bonus question of 'can we do it?'" (2022-06-03). The 99 "should we" questions are pure taste-work. Five years before the explicit naming, Agam had already framed the PM job as 99% taste, 1% execution at the question level.

By 2024, the language started shifting toward the boundary. A Collaborative Article comment: "Building and measuring products is not difficult now. Learning to delight is the next pitstop." That is taste-over-execution without the term: execution (build/measure) is no longer the constraint; the constraint is discrimination of what counts as delight.

The explicit naming arrived with the 2026-04-09 post: "Spec > Sprint / Taste > Execution / Context > Prompt." Per Agam's own framing (C3, 2026-04-26 taste-pass): vocabulary upgrade, not new realization. The April 2026 post is the language-arrival moment, not the insight-arrival moment. Same disposition, sharper words.

## Refinement arc

This belief does not supersede an earlier stated belief. It names a disposition that was being practiced under different vocabulary: the PM metaphor stack (2021-22), the 99/1 grind (2022), the delight-as-next-pitstop framing (2024), the anti-customization declaration (2025-12-04).

Looking back, these stack. At the time, each was its own post. The pattern is retrospective, not a deliberate arc (per C2 taste-pass decision).

**Trilogy relationship (2026-04-09):** Taste > Execution is one of three sibling claims published the same day. Spec > Sprint addresses the process layer (what you write before building). Taste > Execution addresses the craft layer (what you discriminate worth building). Context > Prompt addresses the tooling layer (what you give the model). Three views of one structural claim: the load-bearing work has migrated from doing to deciding. None is parent to the others.
