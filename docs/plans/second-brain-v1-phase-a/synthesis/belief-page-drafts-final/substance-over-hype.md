# Substance over hype

## Statement

When a new category arrives with a new label, the first move is to name what it actually is at the layer below. Blockchain is a database innovation first. GenAI is a platform revolution because it radically reduces the cost of cognition. Agents are LLMs with tools and memory. If the substrate is genuinely new, the excitement is earned. If the substrate is a renamed version of something already understood, the hype gets refused - in writing, regardless of financial exposure to the category being evaluated. This disposition has been applied across four distinct hype cycles spanning eight years, and the pattern of response has not changed.

## Origin

The first visible instance is a February 2018 LinkedIn post: "Blockchain = Gamification decentralized." One line, no theory, just a substrate rename applied to the hottest category of the year. At the time, Agam was running V2 Games and actively selling blockchain product. The post is not outside-critic commentary. It is inside-the-category honesty.

Two weeks later, a fuller crystallization arrived in two posts three days apart. The March 4 post locked the phrase "a database innovation first" - the load-bearing reduction that recurs for eight years. The March 6 post named the cognitive failure driving the hype: "No one wants to accept the biggest database innovation as just a database innovation. Same as saying I will buy an orange not because I need an orange, but because someone else needs it." That post is the canonical statement. It names both the substrate test and the reason the market refuses it.

## Refinement arc

The disposition does not change across hype cycles. What changes is the surface it gets applied to.

By 2024, the same move appears on AI tooling. A March 2024 post on the Devin coding agent introduced a new rhetorical form: "I would catch on to the hype once Devin replaces its founders to build Devin 2.0." This is the substrate test stated as a conditional - withhold belief until the capability claim can pass an observable test.

A July 2024 post made the test explicit as a general principle: "Technology that radically reduces the cost of something" is the definition of a platform revolution. The post also contains the first self-callback to 2018 blockchain: "I am looking at you Blockchain" - confirming the through-line is intentional, not coincidence.

By late 2025, the disposition reached PM craft itself. A December 2025 post on anti-customization applied the same substrate test to B2B SaaS product design: the hype-word "super customizable" hides a six-month implementation tax. Strip it back, refuse the frame.

By April 2026, the cycle compressed to thirty minutes. Agam tried and dropped a generative design tool (Google Stitch) in under half an hour: "When you have already spent hours speccing every pixel, a generative tool gives you a worse version of what you have already decided." The Spec > Sprint / Taste > Execution / Context > Prompt trilogy followed from that single afternoon.
